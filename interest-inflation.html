How are Inflation rates connected to interest rates!

The interest rate is the amount a lender charges a borrower (usually money) and is a percentage of the principal,the amount loaned. Inflation and interest rates tend to move in the same direction because interest rates are the primary tool used by the Federal Reserve, the Canada’s central bank, to manage inflation. Since 2012, the Federal Reserve has targeted annual inflation of 2%. For Example, if we want to take loan, we want the Interest rate to be low. In this case, the Interest rate is the amount of money you must pay back with the actual money in loan you borrowed. The faster you pay back money, the better. The interest rate will double each year until you pay back the money. On the other hand, if you want to save money, then the high interest rate means you can earn more on your savings. (Since there are high interest rates people will spend less money and not buy loans). When the central banks raise interest rates, they try to control inflation. A rise in interest rates from a central bank means that a commercial bank will earn more on their reserves(savings). They might make more from keeping their money in a central bank than lending it out. If they do lend it out, they’ll raise their interest rates to decrease the inflation rates. In conclusion, the rates differ in terms of increasing and decreasing over time to balance the economy.
